üèÜ The "Golden Rule" of TSM

Always take Naltrexone one hour before your first drink of the day, every day you plan to drink.

This timing is essential. It ensures that the Naltrexone has enough time to bind to your opioid receptors before alcohol-induced endorphins are released. If you drink without taking Naltrexone, you risk reinforcing the old, addictive pathways in your brain, which can hinder the extinction process.

It's important to remember that TSM is not about making you sick if you drink, nor is it about forcing abstinence. It's about gradually reducing your desire for alcohol. This means you can still enjoy a drink if you choose, but over time, you'll likely find yourself drinking less, with less frequency, and with less intensity.

What to Expect on Your TSM Journey

The journey with TSM is unique for everyone, but there are common patterns. In the initial phases, you might not notice a dramatic change in your drinking habits. However, many people soon begin to experience subtle shifts:

  • Reduced Cravings: The intense urge to drink may lessen over time.
  • Less Interest: You might find yourself less interested in finishing a drink, or not wanting that second or third drink as much.
  • Fewer Drinking Days: Over weeks and months, the number of days you drink, and the amount you consume on those days, typically decreases.
  • Natural Progression: The process feels natural rather than forced, as your brain gradually "unlearns" the alcohol-reward association.

Patience is Key

This is a gradual process of "unlearning." It takes time for your brain to rewire itself, so consistency with Naltrexone is key. Be patient with yourself, and celebrate small victories along the way.
